The Environmental Impetus

Why KCTF Was Founded

In Ghana, clean and safe running water is a critical gap. The Western Region, despite heavy tropical rains, faces unique ground water contamination challenges. Industrial operations, artisanal mining, and dense urbanization pollutes local rivers, placing schoolgoing children at high risk of water-borne illnesses.

When primary schools lack safe toilets or borehole taps, girls face absenteeism, dropouts rise, and overall community productivity crashes. KCTF was born out of this immediate need to build durable, self-contained community structures protecting environmental health.

The Water Gap in Ghana

● 15% of rural children walk over 3km daily to retrieve turbid creek water.
● 60% of hospital admissions in rural clinics trace back to water contamination.
● 100% Preventable: Proper drilling setups coupled with sand bio-filters completely resolve these infectious threats.

Our Founding History

From Academic Vision to Life-Saving Practice

KCTF was initiated in early 2024 by Samuel Korankye, a computer science student at Takoradi Technical University. Observing the critical public health hurdles faced by farming villages just outskirts of Takoradi, Samuel resolved to organize students, regional hydro-engineers, and community leaders to fund and build low-cost bio-sand filtration containers.

What began as a grass-roots, student-led green movement rapidly expanded. Drawing upon clean environmental designs, the initiative won backing from regional councils and became an incorporated Environmental NGO. Over several short years, KCTF has successfully deployed dozens of deep water pumps, composting toilets, and solar treatment arrays, cementing our status as a trusted development actor.

Our Vision

To engineer a Ghana where safe, bacteria-free drinking water and healthy bio-sanitation assets are accessible to every home, school, and public market, laying down a strong groundwork for economic freedom.

🚀

Our Mission

To design and construct sustainable solar-powered water infrastructure, install community compost toilets, run environmental health workshops, and implement agro-vocational systems.

KCTF Strategic Objectives (2024 - 2028)

✔

Deploy and maintain 50 deep solar boreholes in rural primary school borders across Ghana.

✔

Deliver safe bio-compost public toilet infrastructure to 5 highly-congested municipal markets.

✔

Empower 2,000 women agricultural traders through green composting and bio-sand safety training.

✔

Maintain an active community-led sanitation audit framework, ensuring operational resilience.
